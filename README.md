# JQR Mininova Noise/Drone Sounds

![Synth](https://img.shields.io/badge/Synth-Novation%20Mininova-purple)
![Style](https://img.shields.io/badge/Style-Drone%20%2F%20Noise-orange)


Inspired by drone machines such as **Grendel Drone Commander**, **Casper Drone Lab V2**, and **Analogue Solutions Vostok / Matrixsynth**, this patch collection turns the Novation Mininova into an auto-generative drone and noise structure.

Turn the **Latch** button on, play a note or a chord, and let the sound evolve over time. Every controller is programmed for a fully performance-oriented experience, giving you control over nearly every sound variation.

To better understand which parameters are affected by the **Tweak Controls** and **Animate Pads**, please read the notes below and check the patches with the Mininova editor. Feel free to modify and re-patch everything to create your own drone machine.

Be aware that due to the extensive use of LFOs, the **Tempo** potentiometer becomes an essential tool to control the expression of every patch. As a starting point, set the Tempo to **90 BPM**.

Also note that these patches are very noisy and loud, so turn the **Master Volume** down to **0** before trying any of them. With the minimum volume, play a note in Latch mode and slowly increase the Master Volume.

Some Animate Pads will increase the general volume due to distortions; this is a deliberate dynamic effect. **Do not try any patch wearing headphones.**

### In/back
To make this patch work, physically connect the **Left output** to the **External Input** using a normal jack cable (TS or TRS).

Turn the **Master Volume** to `0` and set the **Audio Input Gain** to `+10` (`Menu > Audio In > InptGain`). Now play a note in Latch mode and slowly increase the Master Volume.

As soon as you hear the feedback growing, adjust the Master Volume until you find a sound you like. Let it build even more and enjoy the total saturation.
